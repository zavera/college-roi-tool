Frequently asked questions

What is FAFSA and why does it matter?

FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) is the form families file to qualify for federal grants, subsidized loans, and work-study programs. Most colleges also use your FAFSA data to calculate their own institutional aid offers. Filing it correctly — and understanding what comes back — is the single most important step in paying for college.

What's the difference between a grant and a loan?

A grant is free money you never repay — like the Pell Grant or an institutional scholarship. A loan is borrowed money you must repay with interest after graduation. Many financial aid award letters intentionally mix the two together, which can make an expensive school look more affordable than it really is. Astra separates them clearly.

What is a financial gap?

Your financial gap is the difference between what college costs and what your grants, scholarships, and loans cover. It's the amount your family needs to come up with through savings, income, or additional borrowing — and it's often much larger than families expect.
